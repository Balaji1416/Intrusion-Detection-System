About Intrusion

Intrusion Detection Systems (IDS) are security mechanisms designed to monitor network traffic and system activities for malicious behavior or policy violations. These systems analyze data patterns, detect anomalies, and alert administrators about potential threats like unauthorized access, malware, or cyberattacks. IDS can be categorized into Network-based IDS (NIDS) and Host-based IDS (HIDS), where NIDS monitors network traffic and HIDS examines activities on individual devices. Some IDS solutions incorporate machine learning and AI to enhance threat detection accuracy and reduce false alarms. By deploying an IDS, organizations can strengthen their cybersecurity defenses and respond proactively to security breaches
